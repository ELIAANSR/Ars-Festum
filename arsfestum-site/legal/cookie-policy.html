Cookie Policy

Last updated 25 June 2026

This policy explains what cookies are, which are used on arsfestum.com (the “Site”), and how you can manage them. It should be read together with our Privacy Policy.

1 · What cookies are

A cookie is a small text file that a website stores on your device when you visit it. Cookies allow the Site to remember your actions and preferences over a period of time. Similar technologies, such as local storage, work in the same way and are covered by this policy.

2 · Cookies we use

The Site is built to be light and currently uses only what is strictly necessary, plus a record of your cookie choice. It does not use advertising cookies, and does not load third-party analytics or tracking by default.

Strictly necessary

  • af_cookie_consent — stores your response to the cookie notice so it is not shown again. Stored in your browser’s local storage. Duration: until cleared or 12 months. No consent required.

Third-party services

The Site loads typefaces from Google Fonts. This may involve a request to Google’s servers to deliver the font files; Google may process your IP address for that purpose. No fonts cookie is set for advertising. If you prefer, fonts can be self-hosted to avoid this request; we will update this section if that change is made.

If analytics or maps are added later

Should the Site introduce analytics, embedded maps, video or social features in future, the relevant cookies will be listed here and, where required, loaded only after you give consent through the cookie notice.

3 · Managing your cookies

You can accept or decline non-essential cookies through the notice shown when you first visit the Site, and you can change browser settings at any time to block or delete cookies. The help pages of your browser explain how to do this. Blocking strictly necessary items may affect how the Site remembers your preferences.

4 · Consent

By continuing to use the Site after making a choice in the cookie notice, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. You may withdraw consent at any time by clearing the stored choice in your browser, after which the notice will be shown again.
